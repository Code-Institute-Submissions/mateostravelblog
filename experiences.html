My Experiences

My story starts in a town called Boynton Beach, Flroida, USA. After my birth, my parents moved back to their hometown of Urireo, Guanajuato, Mexico. I was raised in Urireo until the age of seven. During my time in Urireo, I was able to pick up the basics and core stucture of Spanish. My time in Urireo has shaped my view on the world. This blog sheds light on those experiences.

As you read into my travel blog, you'll get a snapshot of how I have been shaped by the places I've visited. From Berlin, to Belgium, and the Czech Republic, every single country has left it's mark on how I now view the world. Check it out!

My Trips

I visited Belgium in 2015 while I was studying abroad. I planned the trip to Brussels, Belgium, with a few friends. All of the expenses were paid, and all that was left to do was hop on the plane. A week before the trip, the unimaginable occurred. In November of 2015, the Paris Terror Attacks brought the world to a halt. A group of merciless terrorists killed over 100 people in one single night. At the time, I was in Berlin, studying abroad.

With the fear of getting caught in an anti-refugee demonstration (which did occur once in Prague), mixed with the anxiety of a copycat terrorist attack, the trip to Belgium almost got canceled. Somehow, someway, I managed to convince my parents that it was safe to travel to Brussels, Belgium. I told my parents that the only way those terrorists would win is if we let them instill fear into our hearts. I was young and determined to live my life. All of my friends did the same. The trip was still on!

On arrival to Brussels, Belgium, I met up with my friends to visit some monuments and to try the world-famous Belgian waffles! All of the hype is no lie -- the waffles were delicious!

To end the night, we visited the renowned Delirium Café, a bar where you can try over 2000 beers from all across the world. Some of the beer brands we tried were unpronounceable, but it left us wanting more! The café was a fantastic experience.

On our way back to our hostel, we were met with an eerie sight. The Belgian army was patrolling the streets in their riot gear and military tanks. The view of the military in the streets was an eye-opening experience. In the morning, we learned that the mastermind behind the Paris Terror Attacks was a Belgian national who was likely planning an attack in Brussels. Everyone at the hostel was on edge. We were a target due to the vast number of Americans in one central location. The Belgian government put the entire city on a level 5 terror alert.

As we were all thinking our trip was over, one of my friends got a call from an aunt that lived nearby. She offered to drop us off in Waterloo, Brussels, to salvage our trip. And boy, was she right. The trip turned out even better than I expected. We visited the beautiful Mémorial Waterloo 1815. I climbed the 226 steps to get to the top of the Lion's Mound, which gave me an incomparable view of the Waterloo battlefield.

Undoubtedly, Belgium was a fantastic experience. The roadblocks on the trip were just temporary for my friends and me, but for the people of Belgium, the potential risk of a threat to their lives remains very genuine.

My trip to the Czech Republic was very short, but it left me feeling like I visited Europe during the Middle Ages. The architecture of the buildings is so unique and breathtaking. If there is one city in all of my trips that I felt truly represented what I imaged Europe to look and feel like, it would be Prague.

During my brief trip to the city, I visited the Prague Castle, one of the most iconic landmarks in Prague. It is said to be one of the largest castles in the world. As you enter the castle, you are hit with a surge of colors and patterns from the various murals, sculptures, and pathways. The St. Vitus Cathedral within the Prague Castle was enormous and contained striking Neo-Gothic architecture.

My life changed when I discovered the delectable trdelník! It is a sweet bread, in the shape of a cone, that is baked over burning coal and usually served with ice cream. What’s incredible is that the trdelník is a street food available at most corners! Check out my travel tips to see a gif of the trdelník being made.

While in Germany, I lived at the CIEE Berlin Campus located at Gneisenaustraße 27, 10961 Berlin, Germany. I was lucky to be the first class of students to attend the brand new Berlin campus. It was also my first time living in dorms. I was excited about all of the unique experiences that were coming my way. Berlin did not disappoint - it exceeded my expectations.

From the rich history to the delicious bratwurst and doner kebabs, Germany has so much to offer! Berlin especially was a place of its own. Berlin gave me a very young yet laidback vibe. The area I lived in was called Kreuzberg. It was home to many students and a high population of Turkish-German residents. My local barber was Turkish, and although we could not communicate at all, he knew exactly how to cut my hair. I have yet to find someone that cuts my hair as well as he did!

I left Germany with many amazing memories and an urge to return in the near future. Who knows, I might go back one day to live in that beautiful country!

Mexico, my home far away from home. Whenever I’m available, I try to visit Mexico every December with my dad. Mexico, during the winter holiday season is very beautiful. The markets are out and the fair comes into town.

I grew up in a small town called Urireo, Guanajuato, Mexico. It’s located about 4 hours North West of Mexico City. Both my mother and father originate from Urireo. Urireo has a very humble and calming atmosphere. The people are sweet and kind. The culture is very family oriented and engraved in Catholic roots. Most homes in Urireo were built on the slanted hills and valleys. When you enter a typical home in Urireo, the first thing you notice is a small courtyard without a roof. These courtyards usually contain a pila para lavar or in English, a pool to wash. A pila is a square shaped tub or pool made out of concrete. The pilas are filled with water and used to wash dished and sometimes clothes.

You might also notice that since the homes are on the side of hills, you have to fight gravity when standing by swaying forward. The people are used to their way of life, but as a foreigner, it sometimes strikes me as interesting. But living in the mountain area is not all that tough. You build a lot of leg muscle with the amount of walking you do in Urireo. The mountain scenery is to die for as well.

Apart from Urireo, I love visiting the neighboring cities including Mexico City and Guanajuato. In Mexico City you have the Basílica de Guadalupe and right on the outskirts you have the ancient Aztec Pyramid ruins of San Juan Teotihuacán. All great attractions!

When I visited the United Kingdom, the first thing that caught me by surprise was the exchange rate from US Dollars to Pounds. I knew the Pound was stronger than the US Dollar, but I was taken back by the vast difference. Fortunately, I had saved enough for the trip, but a word to the wise, make sure you have a budget for when traveling to the UK.

On the bright side, my experience in the UK was beyond my wildest dreams. Luckily, I had a group of friends to share the experience within London. One of those friends was well versed in the architecture of the city. He guided us to the various monuments and buildings. We visited the London Eye, the Gherkin, Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, and the Tower Bridge, just to name a few.

But above all, my favorite location that I visited was the Emirates Stadium. Home of Arsenal Football Club! Arsenal has been one of my favorite teams since I was a little boy. Unfortunately, there wasn’t enough time in my trip to stop and watch a game, but I was super excited to visit the Arsenal home. I even got to buy a jersey and meet the team mascot, Gunnersaurus!

To top it all off, yes, I did try the world-famous fish and chips in London, and they did not disappoint!
