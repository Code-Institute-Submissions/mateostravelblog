My Experiences

My story starts in my birth town of Boynton Beach, Flroida, USA. After my birth, my parents moved back to their hometown of Urireo, Guanajuato, Mexico. I was raised in Urireo until the age of seven. During my time in Urireo, I developed a love for exploring. My time in Urireo has shaped my views on the world.

As you read into my travel blog, you'll get a snapshot of how I have been shaped by the places I've visited. From Berlin, to Belgium, and the Czech Republic, every single country has left it's mark on how I now view the world. Check it out!

My Trips

I visited Brussels, Belgium in 2015. A week before the trip, the Paris Terror Attacks brought the world to a halt. At the time, I was in Berlin, studying abroad. I managed to convince my parents that it was safe to travel to Brussels. I was young and determined to live my life despite the terror threats.

On arrival to Brussels, I met up with some friends to visit monuments and to try out the world-famous Belgian waffles! To end the night, we visited the renowned Delirium Village, a bar where you can try over 2000 beers from all across the world!

The next day, we learned of a possible terror attack in Brussels. Luckily, one of my friends had an aunt that lived nearby. To salvage our trip, she offered to drop us off in Waterloo, Brussels. The trip turned out even better than I expected! We visited the Mémorial Waterloo 1815, where I climbed the 226 steps to get to the top of the Lion's Mound!

My trip to the Czech Republic was very short, but it left me feeling like I visited Europe during the Middle Ages. An iconic place to visit is the Prague Castle. It is said to be one of the largest castles in the world. As you enter the castle, you are hit with a surge of colors and patterns from the various murals, sculptures, and pathways. The St. Vitus Cathedral within the Prague Castle was enormous and contained striking Neo-Gothic architecture.

Concerning the stomach, my life changed when I discovered the delectable trdelník! It is a sweet bread, in the shape of a cone, that is baked over burning coal and usually served with ice cream. What’s incredible is that the trdelník is a street food available at most corners! Check out my travel tips to see a gif of the trdelník being made.

While in Germany, I lived and studied at the CIEE Berlin Campus. I was lucky to be part of the first class of students to attend the brand new Berlin Campus. Berlin did not disappoint - it exceeded my expectations.

From the rich history to the delicious bratwurst and doner kebabs, Germany has so much to offer! The area I lived in was called Kreuzberg. It was home to many students and a high population of Turkish-German residents. My local barber was Turkish, and although we could not communicate at all, he knew exactly how to cut my hair!

I left Germany with many amazing memories and an urge to return in the near future. Who knows, I might go back one day to live in that beautiful country!

Mexico, my home far away from home. I grew up in a small town called Urireo, Guanajuato, Mexico. It’s located about 4 hours North West of Mexico City. Both my mother and father originate from Urireo. Urireo has a very humble and calming atmosphere. The people are nurturing and kind. The culture is very family oriented and engraved in Catholic roots.

Apart from Urireo, I love visiting the neighboring cities including Mexico City and Guanajuato. In Mexico City you have the Basílica de Guadalupe and right on the outskirts you have the ancient pyramid ruins of San Juan Teotihuacán. All great attractions!

My experience in the UK was beyond my wildest dreams. Luckily, I had a group of friends to share the experience with while in London. We visited the London Eye, the Gherkin, Big Ben, Buckingham Palace, and the Tower Bridge, just to name a few.

By far, my favorite location was the Emirates Stadium. Home of Arsenal Football Club! Arsenal has been one of my favorite teams since I was a little boy. Unfortunately, there wasn’t enough time in my trip to stop and watch a game, but I was super excited to visit the Emirates Stadium. I even got to buy a jersey and to meet the team mascot, Gunnersaurus!

To top it all off, yes, I did try the world-famous fish and chips in London, and they did not disappoint!
