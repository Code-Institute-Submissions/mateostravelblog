My Experiences

My story starts in a town called Boynton Beach, Flroida, USA. After my birth, my parents moved back to their hometown of Urireo, Guanajuato, Mexico. I was raised in Urireo until the age of seven. During my time in Urireo, I was able to pick up the basics and core stucture of Spanish. My time in Urireo has shaped my view on the world. This blog sheds light on those experiences.

As you read into my travel blog, you'll get a snapshot of how I have been shaped by the places I've visited. From Berlin, to Belgium, and the Czech Republic, every single country has left it's mark on how I now view the world. Check it out!

My Trips

While in Germany, I lived at the CIEE Berlin Campus located at Gneisenaustra√üe 27, 10961 Berlin, Germany. I was lucky to be the first class of students to attend the brand new Berlin campus. It was also my first time living in dorms. I was excited about all of the unique experiences that were coming my way. Berlin did not disappoint - it exceeded my expectations.

From the rich history to the delicious bratwurst and doner kebabs, Germany has so much to offer! Berlin especially was a place of its own. Berlin gave me a very young yet laidback vibe. The area I lived in was called Kreuzberg. It was home to many students and a high population of Turkish-German residents. My local barber was Turkish, and although we could not communicate at all, he knew exactly how to cut my hair. I have yet to find someone that cuts my hair as well as he did!

I left Germany with many amazing memories and an urge to return in the near future. Who knows, I might go back one day to live in that beautiful country!
