What you Should Know Before You Go...

Travel Tips

First thing first, it's okay to be scared.

No doubt exploring the unknown can be a bit intimidating at times. I recall feeling somewhat on edge and always alert during some of my travel experiences. At times I questioned whether I should cancel my entire trip due to external factors out of my control.

In Berlin, I saw the thousands of Syrian refugees that escaped war-torn Syria. In Brussels, I saw armed soldiers with tanks patrol the streets after learning of a possible top-level terror threat. In Prague, I got caught in the middle of an anti-refugee protest during the 2015 refugee crisis. In Mexico, I was always on edge and aware that at any moment, gangs could attempt to high jack the bus or taxi I was on.

There are inevitable threats that you must always stay aware of in the back of your mind. But that does not mean you should let these setbacks discourage you from exploring your wildest of dreams. The most significant piece of advice I could give to someone new to traveling is always to do your research, avoid regions of conflict, and always travel with a buddy.

Here are a few more tips that may help you along your travels. Of course, everyone is different, and there might be a few exceptions. Contact with more tips you think I should add!

Bring A Buddy Along!

I know it's tempting to explore the world by yourself. By night, I would walk the streets of Berlin alone. It was fun and thrilling. But at times, I felt unsafe, especially if I was walking through eerie dark streets. When possible, take a buddy! Plus, traveling with someone is always more fun.

When Abroad, Drink Up!

Alcohol is usually a part of life for most countries. From the smooth Tequila in Mexico to the richly scented Jägermeister in Germany, each country hosts its unique liquor or beer specialty. If you're a fan, explore what each country has to offer. Remember to bring a bottle home for your friends and family and to always drink responsibly.

Visit a Museum or Two

I always recommend visiting local museums. One of my favorite museums is the Waterloo Memorial Museum of 1815, located in Waterloo, Belgium. It takes you on an in-depth timeline of the Battle of Waterloo. While in Mexico, you can visit the Museum of the Mummies of Guanajuato.

Visit During Christmas

Visting other countries during Christmas is an eyeopening experience! In Germany, there are Christmas markets set up all around the city. You can get some delicious Glühwein - warm sweet red wine that's to die for! In Mexico, you'll find artwork and sculptures of the Nativity of Jesus on every corner.

Forget About Your Diet!

One thing is certain about traveling -- forget about your diet! Don't be afraid to try street food and new pastries. I always hesitant to try Greek food until someone invited me out to a Greek restaurant in Berlin. Now Choriatiki is all I crave! In Prague, I discovered trdelník, as shown in the gif. It's one of the best pastries to ever grace this Earth.

Getting Around Town!

In my early travels I didn't take advantage of the easy to use public transportation -- major regret! Most cities have apps for their public transit. Don't be afraid to take a train or bus across town. When in Europe, I recommend using cheap air services such as Ryan Air -- super cheap tickets!
