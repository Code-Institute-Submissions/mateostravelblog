What you Should Know Before You Go!

First thing first, it's okay to be scared.

There is no doubt that exploring the unknown can be a bit intimidating at times. I recall feeling somewhat on edge and always alert during some of my travel experiences. At times I questioned whether I should cancel my entire trip due to external factors out of my control. In Berlin, I saw first hand the thousands of Syrian refugees that escaped war-torn Syria. In Brussels, I saw armed soldiers with tanks patrol the streets after learning of a possible top-level terror threat. In Prague, I was caught in the middle of an anti-refugee protest during the 2015 refugee crisis. In Mexico, I was always on edge and aware that at any moment, gangs could high jack the bus I was on and steal everyone's possessions.

Unfortunately, that is the world we live in today. There are always possible threats that you must always stay aware of in the back of your mind. But that does not mean you should let these setbacks discourage you from exploring your wildest of dreams. The most significant piece of advice I could give to someone new to traveling is always to do your research, avoid regions of conflict, and always travel with a buddy.

Here are a few more tips that may help you along your travels. Of course, everyone is different, and there might be a few exceptions. Feel free to contact me if there are more tips you think I should add!

Bring A Buddy Along!

I know it's tempting to explore the world by yourself. By night, I would walk the streets of Berlin alone. It was fun and thrilling. But at times, I felt unsafe, especially if I was walking through eerie dark streets. When possible, take a buddy! Plus, traveling with someone is always more fun.

When Abroad, Drink Up!

Alcohol is usually a part of life for most countries. From the smooth Tequila in Mexico to the richly scented Jägermeister in Germany, each country hosts its unique liquor or beer specialty. If you're a fan, explore what each country has to offer. Remember to bring a bottle home for your friends and family and to always drink responsibly.

Visit a Museum or Two

I always recommend visiting local museums. One of my favorite museums is the Waterloo Memorial Museum of 1815, located in Waterloo, Belgium. It takes you on an in-depth timeline of the Battle of Waterloo. While in Mexico, you can visit the Museum of the Mummies of Guanajuato.

Forget About Your Diet!

One thing is certain about traveling -- forget about your diet! Explore the various dishes each country has to offer! Don't be afraid to try street food and new pastries. I always hesitant to try Greek food until someone invited me out to a Greek restaurant in Berlin. Now Choriatiki is all I think about! In Prague, I was introduced to the trdelník, as shown in the gif above. Probably one of the best pastries to ever grace this Earth.

Getting Around Town!

In my early travels I did not take advantage of all the easy to use public transportation -- major regret! Most cities have apps for their public transit. Don't be afraid to take a train or bus across town. Always be vigilant for pickpocketers, and keep your belongings on you at all times. When in Europe, I recommend using cheap air services such as Ryan Air -- super cheap tickets!
